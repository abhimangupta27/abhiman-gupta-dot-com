About

I'm a PhD student in the College of Optical Sciences at the University of Arizona (GPA 3.9), where I completed rotations in biomedical optics, polarization-sensitive OCT, and selective illumination microscopy. I hold a BS in Biomedical Engineering from the same institution.

My research spans photoacoustic imaging, fluorescence microscopy, and optical system design. I'm particularly interested in building imaging tools that bridge the gap between benchtop optics and clinical utility.

Outside the lab, I'm an award-winning photographer, a former startup entrepreneur-in-residence, and a teaching assistant recognized for outstanding mentorship. I previously interned at Alcon Research.
